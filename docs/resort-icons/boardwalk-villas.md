## Story Read

BoardWalk Villas should feel like the home-base version of Disney's BoardWalk: still connected to the lively turn-of-the-century Atlantic City/Coney Island mood, but calmer, more tucked in, and more residential than BoardWalk Inn.

Disney's pool detail page gives the clearest villa-specific distinction. Luna Park Pool is shared BoardWalk energy: carnival-themed pool, 200-foot-long Keister Coaster waterslide, 1920s boardwalk amusement-park feeling, elephant water-spout statues, and vintage show-poster atmosphere. The villa-side differentiator is the pair of leisure pools: one in the Rose Garden Courtyard and one next to the Community Hall building.

The user-loved hook is that BoardWalk Villas lets a guest step between two feelings: a private-feeling villa courtyard and Community Hall home base, then the waterfront boardwalk, Surrey bikes, Crescent Lake paths, and Luna Park energy just outside.

## Icon Direction

- Softer villa facade with balcony/window rhythm to separate it from the Inn's marquee-heavy hotel face.
- Rose Garden Courtyard leisure pool as the main water shape.
- Pennant/flag cue for Community Hall as the villa home-base landmark.
- Small rose/leaf accent to make the courtyard idea legible.
- Crescent Lake water and boardwalk planks to keep the EPCOT resort-area setting.
- Smaller Keister Coaster arc as a shared Luna Park echo, not the main identity.

## Motion Notes

- Courtyard water drifts gently on hover.
- Community Hall pennant lifts slightly.
- Rose accent rises just a touch.
- Small Keister Coaster echo glides forward.
- Existing sparkle motion adds the magical card feel; reduced-motion disables transitions.
