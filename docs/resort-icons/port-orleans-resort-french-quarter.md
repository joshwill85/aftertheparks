# Disney's Port Orleans Resort - French Quarter Icon

Figma component: https://www.figma.com/design/S8XwSQh6krHV3LrecKw3nF?node-id=40-76

## Story Anchors

- New Orleans French Quarter first-read: warm courtyard color, cobblestone/gas-lamp feeling, wrought-iron balcony rail, and a magnolia bloom.
- Mardi Gras energy through gold, green, and purple bead strands, with the icon kept festive rather than noisy.
- Doubloon Lagoon is the emotional center, with water filling the base of the mark.
- Scales, the 51-foot sea-serpent waterslide, is the dominant silhouette so the resort reads as the French Quarter pool instead of a generic New Orleans scene.
- King Neptune and doubloon language appear as a small gold coin/trident cue near Scales.
- The alligator jazz band is represented as a tiny musical/alligator cue at the bottom edge.
- Sassagoula River movement and shared recreation are represented through soft water ribbons, with South Quarter Games and surrey bikes kept as supporting notes rather than main symbols.

## Design Notes

The icon should feel like a tiny Mardi Gras courtyard postcard that has come alive at the pool. Scales is intentionally oversized because it is the strongest resort-specific shape; the balcony, lamp, magnolia, beads, river ribbons, alligator jazz cue, and King Neptune doubloon make the story richer without stealing the first read. Hover motion is restrained: the lagoon ripples, Scales leans forward, beads settle, and the little jazz/spark details lift.
