Disney's own resort page frames the Contemporary around the iconic A-frame tower,
the Resort Monorail breezing through the tower, spectacular views, Bay Lake pools,
and the 90-foot Mary Blair Grand Canyon / American Southwest mural inside the
Grand Canyon Concourse.

## Guest-Loved Theme Read

The emotional hook is not just "modern hotel." It is motion and proximity:
guests love that the monorail physically passes through the building, that the
resort feels connected to Magic Kingdom, and that the open concourse has a
large-scale Mary-Blair color story that makes the architecture feel playful
instead of cold.

## Icon Direction

The icon uses four resort-specific symbols:

- A-frame tower silhouette for instant resort recognition.
- Monorail car through the center for the "alive" moment.
- Mary-Blair-inspired geometric blocks in the concourse window.
- Bay Lake reflection at the bottom to ground the tower in place.

The card version animates gently on hover/focus: the monorail glides, the water
shifts, and the small sun/spark turns slightly. Reduced-motion users receive the
static icon.
