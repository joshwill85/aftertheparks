## Story Read

Coronado Springs should feel like a warm lakeside resort shaped by Spanish, Mexican, and Southwest design influences: Lago Dorado, the Casitas/Ranchos/Cabanas settlements, Gran Destino Tower's modern Spanish ribbon-like presence, and the Dig Site as the playful centerpiece.

Disney's pool detail page gives the clearest resort-specific visual anchor. The Dig Site is home to the Lost City of Cibola pool, a Mayan-ruins themed complex with a 50-foot pyramid centerpiece, water streaming down stone steps, and a 123-foot waterslide with a spitting Jaguar spirit animal. The same page also calls out the 3 leisure pools at the Casitas, Ranchos, and Cabanas settlements.

The user-loved hook is the contrast: Coronado can feel polished and grown-up around Gran Destino and Lago Dorado, then suddenly adventurous and family-friendly at the pyramid pool. The icon needs both halves, not only the pyramid.

## Icon Direction

- Lost City of Cibola pyramid as the primary silhouette.
- Lago Dorado water and golden shore as the base.
- Jaguar waterslide and spray as the playful motion cue.
- Gran Destino ribbon tower behind the pyramid to keep the newer resort identity visible.
- Three small color tiles for Casitas, Ranchos, and Cabanas.
- Warm sun/spark accents for a magical Southwest evening feel.

## Motion Notes

- Gran Destino ribbon lifts and rotates slightly.
- Pyramid rises one pixel on hover.
- Waterfall drops subtly down the stone steps.
- Jaguar slide cue moves forward.
- Settlement tiles shift gently; reduced-motion disables transitions.
